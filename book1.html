Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind

"Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind" (Hebrew: קיצור תולדות האנושות, Qitzur Toldot ha-Enoshut) is a book by Yuval Noah Harari, first published in Hebrew in Israel in 2011 based on a series of lectures Harari taught at The Hebrew University of Jerusalem, and in English in 2014. The book, focusing on Homo sapiens, surveys the history of humankind, starting from the Stone Age and going up to the twenty-first century. The account is situated within a framework that intersects the natural sciences with the social sciences. This is a philosophical book of grand scale and wide perspective, which tells the story of the development of mankind and why it is mankind rather than other creatures who have become the masters of the earth at four levels: the cognitive revolution, the agrarian revolution, the integration of mankind, and the scientific and technological revolution.
